Source: Tuberfilter Article

  • Costco operates on a membership-based warehouse model, requiring customers to pay an annual fee (about $65 in the U.S.) to shop, which helps drive customer loyalty and repeat purchases.
  • The company runs nearly 900 warehouses across 14 countries and sells groceries, electronics, apparel, bakery items, and services like gas stations and optical centers.
  • In 2024, Costco generated around $250 billion in global net sales and had about 137 million members worldwide, making it one of the largest retailers globally.

Source: Statista

Costco is a membership based warehouse retailer that built its reputation on pricing and bulk savings. Costco’s biggest appeal is value. Most of their items are sold in large quantities, which lowers the per unit cost and makes it attractive for anyone who can store and use bulk products. Their private label, Kirkland Signature, is also a major part of Costco’s appeal as many of its products are known for quality that rivals or even matches other brands at a lower price point. Customer service and return policies are generally considered pretty good and known for being fairly flexible with returns. One of the most best parts of the Costco experience is the food court. The $1.50 hot dog and soda combo. It’s been priced the same for decades and the symbol of Costco.
